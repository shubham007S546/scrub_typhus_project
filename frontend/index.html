About ScrubDx AI

System architecture, clinical basis, and development methodology

🦠 What is Scrub Typhus?

Scrub typhus is an acute zoonotic febrile illness caused by Orientia tsutsugamushi, transmitted by infected chigger mite larvae. It is the most common rickettsial infection in the Indian subcontinent, with epidemics reported across sub-Himalayan states including Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, and the northeast.

Untreated mortality can reach 70%. With early doxycycline treatment, prognosis is excellent. The pathognomonic eschar (black scab at bite site) is present in only 13–18% of Indian patients, making clinical diagnosis challenging.

⚠️ Clinical Disclaimer

This AI system is intended for clinical decision support only. It does not replace physician judgment. Diagnosis should be confirmed by IgM ELISA serology (InBios or equivalent). In endemic areas with high clinical suspicion, empirical doxycycline therapy should be initiated without waiting for laboratory confirmation, as early treatment dramatically reduces mortality.

💊 Treatment Summary (WHO-Endorsed)

First-line: Doxycycline 100 mg twice daily × 5–7 days (uncomplicated), × 10–14 days (complicated). Pregnancy / intolerance: Azithromycin 500 mg once daily × 5 days. Severe disease: IV Azithromycin + ICU supportive care. Early treatment is the single most important factor in reducing complications and mortality.
